Ellipse (Horizontal, a > b)

Standard Diagram

Diagram showing center, vertices, foci, axes, and latus recta.

Definition

An ellipse is the locus of a point such that the sum of its distances from two fixed points (foci) is constant.

Standard Properties

  • Standard Equation: x²/a² + y²/b² = 1
  • Center: (0, 0)
  • Eccentricity: e = √((a² − b²)/a²)
  • Vertices: (±a, 0)
  • Foci: (±ae, 0)
  • Distance between foci: 2ae
  • Directrices: x = ±a/e

Axes

  • Major Axis: y = 0, length = 2a
  • Minor Axis: x = 0, length = 2b

Latus Recta

  • Length of Latus Rectum: 2b² / a
  • Equations of Latus Recta: x = ±ae

Focal Distances

  • Sum of distances from foci (SP + S′P): 2a
  • Distance between directrices: 2a / e

Parametric Coordinates

(x, y) = (a cosθ, b sinθ)

Shifted Center Form

(x − h)²/a² + (y − k)²/b² = 1
